High School Website: A Peer-to-Peer Academic Support Hub

This project was one of my earliest ventures into web development, born from a desire to help fellow students. Developed with a friend, it was a direct response to a shared challenge: the lack of a single, organized platform for study materials.

The Challenge

During my early high school years, my classmates and I faced a common frustration: study materials, exam information, and academic resources were completely decentralized. Students had to search fragmented sources or rely on word-of-mouth to find sample questions, making exam preparation inefficient and stressful. We lacked a single, reliable hub for academic support.

My Solution

As a response, a friend and I took the initiative to build the "High School Website." We developed a simple, centralized web platform designed specifically for our fellow students. The site served as a peer-to-peer resource center that organized and provided:

  • Curated Exam Materials: We gathered and shared sample questions and key topic outlines for upcoming exams across all grades (9-12).
  • School Analytics & Guides: We offered straightforward analyses and advice on navigating our school's curriculum and academic expectations.

The platform successfully transformed a fragmented system into a single, accessible hub, making it easier for students to find the help they needed to succeed.

My Takeaway

While not a complex system by today's standards, this project was a critical first step. It taught me the fundamentals of project collaboration, user-focused design, and the immense value of taking initiative. It cemented my interest in building practical tools that solve real-world problems for a community.

Technologies Used

  • HTML5 (Semantic)
  • CSS3 (Flexbox, Grid, Animations)
  • JavaScript (tsParticles library)
  • Git & GitHub Pages (Version Control & Deployment)

Project Goals
