THE STORY

Tanzimat Authors Project

2023-2024 (12th Grade) Istanbul, Turkey
Academics & Literature

In my 12th-grade literature class, I volunteered for a year-long project that I was truly passionate about. My friend and I were tasked with researching the authors of the Tanzimat period by visiting their actual homes and museums.

Walking Through History

We visited the homes of many great authors: Ahmet Hamdi Tanpınar, Sait Faik Abasıyanık, Orhan Kemal, Tevfik Fikret (Aşiyan Museum), Hüseyin Rahmi Gürpınar, Reşat Nuri Güntekin, and Mehmet Akif Ersoy. We also visited Orhan Pamuk's "Museum of Innocence."

Many find visiting museums boring, but for me, this was profound. To see the environment where these authors lived and wrote gave me a deeper understanding of their work. I realized how much their surroundings inspired them; they wrote by observing the world they lived in.

Writers as Collectors

One of the most fascinating discoveries was that these writers were also incredible collectors. At the Museum of Innocence, I was stunned to see a giant display of thousands of cigarette butts, each one smoked by a character in the novel, collected by the author.

This level of dedication—collecting thousands of photos, old watches, and all kinds of objects, filling a five-story building—showed me that writing is another level of existence. It inspired me to, one day, commit to writing books and poetry myself.

The Final Project

We compiled our research, photos, and documents over several months. We also produced a YouTube video where I presented our findings. Our teacher loved the final project and gave us a full score.

At first, everyone thought we had chosen the "hardest" project. But in the end, it was the most rewarding. I have zero regrets and am grateful for an experience that truly enriched my understanding of literature.
