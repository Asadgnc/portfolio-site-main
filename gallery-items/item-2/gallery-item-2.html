THE STORY

The Invisible Rival & The Deciding Decimal

June 2021 School Auditorium
Achievements

The air in the auditorium was stifling, but it wasn't the heat making me sweat—it was the weight of a year-long mission resting on my shoulders.

Our school system was unique: boys and girls attended separate classes. While this might sound trivial, it fundamentally altered the psychology of competition for me. My main rival—the top student from the girls' class—was an enigma. I never saw her raise her hand or solve a problem on the board.

The Invisible Competition

Human psychology is fascinating: when you can't see your opponent, your mind idealizes them. Whenever I felt tired and reached to close my book, an inner voice would whisper: "She is studying right now. Can you afford to sleep?" It became less of a race and more like shadowboxing against a perfectionist version of myself.

Then, the announcement came: 93.8375 points.

Note the precision. Not 93, not 94. The victory was decided by that tiny 0.0375 margin. It wasn't luck; it was the mathematical proof of a battle fought for every single decimal point.

The Moment of Release

Walking onto the stage, I felt my knees shaking. Most probably mistook it for stage fright. In reality, it was my nervous system finally decompressing after a year of rigid discipline. It wasn't fear; it was the physical release of pressure.

That day, I learned that the toughest competition is the one where you cannot see the scoreboard until the very end.
