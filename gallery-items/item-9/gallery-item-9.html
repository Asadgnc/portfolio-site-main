THE STORY

Unveiling the Hidden Past: Historical Sessions with Mustafa Armağan

2022 (11th Grade) Municipal Cultural Center & Private Home Library
History & Learning

In 11th grade, a friend and I seized a rare intellectual opportunity. Our municipality announced a 6-week exclusive seminar series titled "History Talks," held every Friday evening. The key attraction was the speaker: **Mustafa Armağan**, one of Turkey's most prolific, influential, and controversial historians. As the Editor-in-Chief of **"Derin Tarih" (Deep History)** magazine and author of bestsellers like *"History Under the Ashes"* and *"Abdulhamid's Dance with Wolves,"* Mr. Armağan is famous for challenging the "official" republican narrative and offering a neo-Ottoman perspective that rehabilitates figures like Sultan Abdulhamid II.

Friday Nights of Enlightenment

These sessions were far removed from the rote memorization of high school history classes. Each seminar lasted at least 3 to 4 hours, yet the intensity never waned. Mr. Armağan didn't just lecture; he presented evidence. Using a projector, he would display archival documents, forgotten photographs, and censored letters, effectively deconstructing the history we thought we knew.

We dove deep into the geopolitical strategies of the late Ottoman Empire, the complexities of the transition to the Republic, and the untold stories of the Gallipoli Campaign. It was an exercise in critical thinking, teaching us to question *who* wrote the history books and *why*.

The Sanctuary of Knowledge: Inside the Library

The culmination of the program was a privilege few get to experience. Mr. Armağan invited the most dedicated participants to his private residence to visit his personal library. Walking into his study was like stepping into a time capsule. The walls were lined floor-to-ceiling with thousands of volumes.

What made this truly breathtaking was his collection of **Rare Books**. We saw original manuscripts and books printed in the **Ottoman Script** (the Perso-Arabic alphabet used before 1928). Holding these yellowed, fragile pages—some centuries old—was a tactile connection to our ancestors. He showed us how he interprets these primary sources to craft his arguments, giving us a masterclass in historiography.

A Lasting Legacy

This experience fundamentally shifted my worldview. I realized that history is not a static monolith but a battlefield of ideas. Mustafa Armağan's passion for uncovering the truth, regardless of controversy, became a source of inspiration for me.

Since then, I have devoured his bibliography. That visit to his library planted a seed of curiosity in me that continues to grow, reminding me always to look for the story "under the ashes."
