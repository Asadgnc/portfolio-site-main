THE STORY

Hammering History: The Final Act of a Valedictorian

June 2024 (12th Grade) High School
Milestone

In June 2024, my high school journey concluded with the highest distinction possible. After four years of relentless dedication, I graduated as the Valedictorian of my class with a GPA of 95.53. This wasn't just another ceremony; it was the crowning moment of my academic career, filled with intense emotions and unforgettable milestones.

The School Log Tradition

That year, our school inaugurated a new tradition: the Valedictorian would hammer a nameplate onto the ceremonial "School Log" (Kütük). Standing on stage next to the Principal, holding the hammer, I felt the weight of hundreds of eyes and camera lenses on me. My hands were trembling so much that I could barely hold the handle steady. But with a deep breath, I struck the nail. That sound wasn't just metal hitting wood; it was the sound of my name being permanently etched into the school's history.

A Stranger's Encouragement

The next challenge was the speech. I had written it months in advance and rehearsed it countless times, but when the moment arrived, my confidence wavered. I retreated to the restroom for a final pep talk in the mirror. A stranger, a parent of another student, saw me rehearsing and asked, "Are you going to speak on stage?" I nodded nervously, "Yes, brother." He smiled and said, "You got this, don't be nervous." That small encouragement from a stranger was the push I needed.

Family Pride

After delivering my speech, the host announced a surprise: my parents were invited to the stage. Seeing them walk up, with tears of joy and pride streaming down their faces, was the most powerful moment of my life. They presented me with a full gold coin—a traditional gift of honor—and we took an iconic photo that captured our shared victory. I had been too nervous to eat any of the food served earlier, but cutting the graduation cake with the Principal tasted like pure success. As we tossed our caps into the air and danced to national songs, I knew I wasn't just ending a chapter; I was celebrating a legacy.
