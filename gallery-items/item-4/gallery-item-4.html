THE STORY

The Power of Words: Lessons from the Literature Club

2022-2023 (11th Grade) School Auditorium & Hallways
Leadership

To many, a Literature Club implies quiet reading or reciting poetry. However, in its essence, a Literature Club is a laboratory for critical thinking and rhetoric. In 11th grade, during the club elections, I ran for president with a specific goal: to transform the club from a passive group into an active force for intellectual engagement.

The Debate Paradox

Our flagship initiative was the inter-class debate tournament. I poured my energy into helping my own class's team, conducting deep research and refining our arguments. We were prepared to win. However, we learned a harsh lesson in the nature of debates: the luck of the draw.

In the finals, we were assigned to defend a deeply illogical and indefensible position. Despite performing objectively better than our opponents in terms of public speaking and strategy, the inherent weakness of our topic cost us the victory. We came in second.

I was heartbroken for my team, but it taught me that sometimes, even the best execution cannot overcome a flawed premise.

Visual & Social Impact

Beyond debates, we revitalized the school's atmosphere through visual storytelling. We curated bulletin boards (panolar) featuring literary analysis, author biographies, and creative writing. These social activities turned the school hallways into spaces of learning.

This experience wasn't just about leadership; it was about understanding the nuances of persuasion, success, and the valuable sting of failure.
