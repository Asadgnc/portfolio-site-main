THE STORY

The Crown of Seven Hills: A Journey Through Art and Engineering at Çamlıca Mosque

Spring 2023 (11th Grade) Büyük Çamlıca Hill, Istanbul
Culture & Events

In the spring of 2023, amidst the blooming beauty of Istanbul, our school organized an excursion to the city's highest peak: Büyük Çamlıca Hill. As the **Student Council President**, I played a key role in coordinating the logistics for hundreds of students, ensuring a smooth flow through this massive 57,000-square-meter complex. My goal was to expose my peers not just to a place of worship, but to a monument of modern engineering and historical consciousness.

The Language of Numbers and Symbols

Upon arrival, the sheer scale of the mosque left us speechless. We learned that this masterpiece was designed by two female architects, Bahar Mızrak and Hayriye Gül Totu, adding a significant layer to its story. The dimensions of the mosque are a code of history:

  • 107.1 Meters: Four of the six minarets stand exactly 107.1 meters tall, commemorating the Victory of Manzikert in 1071, which opened the gates of Anatolia to the Turks.
  • 72 and 34: The main dome soars to 72 meters, symbolizing the 72 nations residing in Istanbul, while its diameter of 34 meters is a nod to the city's license plate code.
  • Six Minarets: These represent the six pillars of Islamic faith (Iman).

Interior: Where Tradition Meets Nanotechnology

Stepping inside, we were enveloped by a sense of peace within a hall capable of holding **63,000 people**. The floor was covered with 17,000 square meters of specially woven, anti-bacterial carpet. What fascinated us most was the engineering: the calligraphy plates were crafted using nanotechnology and stainless steel. The acoustics were flawless, carrying sound clearly to every corner without echo.

We were also told that the building is equipped with advanced seismic isolators, making it one of the safest structures against earthquakes—a designated sanctuary for up to 100,000 people in emergencies.

Museum of Islamic Civilizations & Library

Beneath the mosque lies the **Museum of Islamic Civilizations**, spanning 11,000 square meters. This was an educational treasure trove. We viewed nearly 800 artifacts, including Holy Relics attributed to the Prophet Muhammad, historical Kaaba covers, Ottoman swords, and ancient Qurans. Digital installations brought the scientific history of the Islamic Golden Age to life.

Later, we visited the 3,000-square-meter library. The serene atmosphere and vast collection of books inspired many of us to sit down, study for a while, and appreciate the joy of learning in such a majestic setting.

The City in the Palm of Our Hands

We concluded our visit on the outer terrace, the highest vantage point in Istanbul. From there, the 16-million-person metropolis sprawled out before us. We could see the Bosphorus winding like a ribbon, the bridges connecting Asia and Europe, and the Princes' Islands.

Standing there in the wind, looking out over two continents, we felt a profound connection to the history and future of this magnificent city.
