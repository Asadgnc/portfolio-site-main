THE STORY

Leading Cuma Prayer as Imam

May 2024 (12th Grade) Sahra-i Kebir Mosque, Ümraniye
Leadership & Community

As a volunteer, I was given the incredible opportunity to serve as the Imam for the Cuma (Friday) prayer at Sahra-i Kebir Mosque, one of the largest mosques in Ümraniye. This meant I was responsible for delivering the Friday Khutbah (sermon) and leading the prayer for a congregation of thousands.

A Test of Poise

This was the first time I had ever addressed a crowd of this magnitude. My close friend, Cemil Arslan, stood with me as the Muezzin. Despite the scale, I felt a different kind of excitement—not the "bad" nervousness, but a "good," positive energy.

All my previous experiences—from theater and competitions to the TÜGVA dinner—had built up my public speaking skills and self-confidence. I knew how to hold myself in these situations. It was a milestone I'll never regret, and it made me realize how much I had grown.

An Ongoing Honor

The experience was so positive that my teachers and mentors still occasionally invite me back to lead the Cuma prayer at their mosques. Whenever I find the free time, I am honored to participate in such a meaningful service.
